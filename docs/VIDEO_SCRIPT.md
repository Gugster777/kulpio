# Kulpio — firSTep Project Video (script + shot list)

Target length: **3 minutes** (rules allow 2–4). Upload to YouTube as
**unlisted**. Wear school uniform / follow the dress code. The last 5 seconds
must show the **school logo** and the text **"firSTep Project Competition"**.

> Speak naturally — this script is a skeleton, not a cage. If you say it in
> your own words it will always sound better. Practice twice, then record.

---

## Shot list & script

### 0:00–0:10 — Title card
**Screen:** title slide (use slide 1 of `Kulpio_firSTep.pptx`): "Kulpio —
Waste less food. Save money. In any language."
**Voice-over:**
> "Kulpio — a food-freshness tracker that helps households waste less food."

### 0:10–0:25 — Who I am
**Screen:** you, on camera, uniform, plain background.
**Say:**
> "Hi! My name is Daniil Bejenari, I'm a student at T.L. Orizont in
> Ceadîr-Lunga, and this is my project for the firSTep Project Competition,
> in the Creative Engineers category."

### 0:25–0:50 — The problem (purpose)
**Screen:** slide 3 (problem stats), then a quick shot of a real fridge —
ideally yours, with something forgotten at the back.
**Say:**
> "About a third of all the food we produce is wasted — and a huge share of
> that happens at home. Not because food spoils too fast, but because we
> *forget* it. We buy it, push it to the back of the fridge, and find it too
> late. That's money in the bin — and needless emissions. My goal was to fix
> the forgetting."

### 0:50–1:50 — The app (method, part 1 — DEMO, the heart of the video)
**Screen:** screen-recording of the real app on your phone. Rehearse this
flow once so it's smooth (seed a few items first):
1. Home dashboard — point at the expiring count, the money-at-risk chip and
   the live countdown ("Yogurt — in 6 hours").
2. Scan a barcode → the product card appears → "To fridge".
3. Photograph a receipt → the review sheet fills the fridge.
4. Freshness rings on the items — green, amber, red.
5. Mark something "Used it" → the money-saved counter moves.
6. Recipes → "cook first what's going off".
7. Shared fridge — the code, members, chat.
8. Flip the language (e.g. Romanian or Russian) — everything translates.
**Say (over the demo):**
> "Kulpio makes tracking food almost effortless. Scan a barcode, photograph a
> label or a whole receipt — the app reads them. Every item wears a freshness
> ring, and the home screen counts down to the next expiry. When something is
> about to go off, Kulpio reminds me and suggests what to cook. When I use
> food in time, it shows the money I saved. My family shares one fridge with
> a single code — with an activity feed and a chat. And it works completely
> offline, on any phone, in thirty-three languages."

### 1:50–2:15 — How it's built (method, part 2)
**Screen:** slide "Under the hood" (architecture), or your editor with the
project open.
**Say:**
> "Technically, the whole app is one static HTML file — no frameworks, no
> build step — installed as a Progressive Web App with a service worker, so
> it runs offline. One Cloudflare Worker serves both the app and its API,
> with an SQLite database for accounts and shared households. Printed
> best-before dates always beat estimates — the app never pretends to know
> more than it does. And over three hundred automated tests run on every
> change, completely offline."

### 2:15–2:40 — Results & the Global Goals
**Screen:** slide with the impact chart (labeled projection), then the SDG
slide.
**Say:**
> "Under a transparent model, Kulpio projects around thirty percent fewer
> wasted items — roughly a hundred and twenty-eight euros saved per household
> per year — and I've designed a small real-world pilot study to measure the
> actual effect. The project serves the UN Global Goals: responsible
> consumption, zero hunger, and climate action."

### 2:40–2:55 — Conclusion & future
**Screen:** you on camera again (or the roadmap slide).
**Say:**
> "Kulpio is free and open-source. Next, I'm bringing it to the Play Store as
> a native Android app and running the pilot with real families. Less wasted
> food, more saved money — in everyone's own language. Thank you!"

### 2:55–3:00 — Outro (REQUIRED)
**Screen:** school logo + the text **"firSTep Project Competition"** (hold
for the full 5 seconds). You can add "Kulpio · Daniil Bejenari · T.L.
Orizont, Ceadîr-Lunga" beneath.

## Recording checklist

- [ ] Phone/camera horizontal, 1080p, steady (prop it or use a tripod).
- [ ] Quiet room; record voice close to the mic; re-record any clip you
      stumble in — short takes are your friend.
- [ ] Screen-record the app demo separately (phone screen recorder), then cut
      it under the voice-over.
- [ ] School uniform / dress code on camera. ✔ rules
- [ ] Title at the start, logo + "firSTep Project Competition" in the last
      5 s. ✔ rules
- [ ] Length between 2:00 and 4:00. ✔ rules
- [ ] Upload as **unlisted** on YouTube; test the link in a private window.
- [ ] Free editors if needed: CapCut (phone) or DaVinci Resolve (PC).

## Demo-prep tip

Before screen-recording, add ~7 realistic items (some expiring today/
tomorrow, some fresh, one frozen), set prices, and open the shared fridge
with a second device or browser tab so the chat has a message or two. A
90-second rehearsal makes the demo look effortless.
